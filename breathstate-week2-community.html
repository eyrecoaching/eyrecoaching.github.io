The Epidemic

Most people breathe
wrong. All day. Every day.

At rest. During sleep. Under stress. They don't know they're doing it. And it is slowly changing their face, their sleep, their health and their children's future.

James Nestor, Stanford University: Six days of enforced mouth breathing produced a 13-point rise in blood pressure, significant HRV drop, and a 4,820% increase in snoring. Six days.

Mouth vs Nose

The wrong airway costs you

👄 Mouth Breathing
Bypasses all nasal filtration — allergens, bacteria, viruses enter unfiltered
Zero nitric oxide production — 18% less oxygen delivered to cells
Dries the airway — increases infection risk and snoring
Elevates cortisol — keeps the nervous system in low-grade threat state
Disrupts sleep architecture — reduces deep sleep significantly
Over years — changes facial structure and narrows the airway
vs
👃 Nasal Breathing
Filters, warms and humidifies every breath before it reaches the lungs
Produces nitric oxide — dilates blood vessels, increases oxygen uptake 18%
Maintains airway moisture — reduces snoring and infection risk
Activates parasympathetic system — signals safety to the nervous system
Supports deep sleep — the breath that heals
Over years — maintains and supports healthy facial structure
Facial Structure

Breathing changes
your face

Dr John Mew's orthotropic research documented structural changes in identical twins — one nasal breather, one mouth breather. Same DNA. Measurably different faces. Different airways. Different long-term health trajectories.

  • Longer, narrower face profile
  • Recessed jaw and chin
  • Crowded and misaligned teeth
  • Narrowed airway — higher sleep apnoea risk
  • Drooping eyelids and flattened cheekbones
📖

Mew, J. — Orthotropics: Identical twin studies show different facial development from breathing pattern alone. Same genetics. Different faces.

A
Mouth Breather Profile
The Adenoid Face
Long, slack jaw. Open mouth at rest. Narrow palate. Crowded teeth. Eyes that appear drooping. Often misdiagnosed as simply needing orthodontics — the breathing pattern is the root cause.
B
Nasal Breather Profile
The Developed Face
Wider palate. Straighter teeth. Defined jawline. Elevated cheekbones. Clearer airway. This is not genetics alone — it is the mechanical result of correct breathing pressure during development.

In adults the structural changes slow. The systemic damage does not. Every night of mouth breathing is a night of disrupted sleep, elevated cortisol, and reduced oxygen delivery.

Kids
For Every Parent Here

Your child's face
is forming right now.

A child's bones are still malleable. The airway is still developing. Mouth breathing in a child is not a minor habit — it is actively reshaping their face, their sleep, their cognitive development, and their long-term health. In real time.

Bonuck et al, Pediatrics (2012): Sleep disordered breathing in children is associated with significant behavioural and neurodevelopmental problems — including symptoms frequently misattributed to ADHD.

Warning Signs

Is your child a
mouth breather?

Most parents have never been told what to look for. These signs are common. They are not normal.

Lips apart at rest — the clearest single indicator
Snoring or noisy breathing during sleep
Restless sleep — tossing, kicking, waking frequently
Dark circles under the eyes — chronic poor sleep quality
Difficulty concentrating at school — brain not recovering overnight
Crowded or misaligned teeth — narrow palate from mouth breathing
Frequent ear, nose and throat infections

Nitric Oxide

What your nose
produces. Your mouth cannot.

Every breath through the nose delivers nitric oxide — a gaseous molecule produced in the nasal sinuses. Every breath through the mouth delivers none. The difference in oxygen uptake is measurable at 18%.

🔬

Lundberg et al (1996): Nasal breathing produces significant nitric oxide in the nasal airways. Mouth breathing produces none. Foundational research confirmed across multiple studies since.

🔬

Nestor, J — Breath (2020): Nasal breathing increases oxygen uptake by approximately 18% compared to mouth breathing through nitric oxide-mediated vasodilation.

What Nitric Oxide Does
🩸
Dilates blood vessels
Widens arteries and capillaries — more blood flow to muscles and organs with every breath
💨
Increases oxygen uptake 18%
The same volume of air delivers significantly more usable oxygen when breathed nasally
🛡️
Antiviral and antibacterial
Actively neutralises pathogens in the nasal passage before they reach the lungs
🧠
Enhances memory and learning
Increases cerebral blood flow — directly linked to cognitive performance and recall
❤️
Regulates blood pressure
The vasodilating effect reduces systemic blood pressure with sustained nasal breathing practice
Sleep & Snoring

Snoring is not normal.
It is a health event.

!
What's happening
Airway obstruction
Mouth breathing during sleep causes the tongue and soft tissues to collapse backward, partially blocking the airway. The body struggles. Sleep architecture fractures. Deep sleep evaporates.
→
The consequences
Nightly damage
Reduced blood oxygen. Elevated cortisol on waking. Disrupted growth hormone release. Increased cardiovascular disease risk over years. Morning fatigue that no amount of coffee fixes.
✓
Tonight
What to do
Clear nasal passage before bed. Sleep on your side. Consider mouth tape — one strip across the centre of the lips. And do Flow 02 before sleep. The practice prepares the airway.

Mouth taping: supported by Nestor, McKeown and Huberman. 20 cents per night. A small piece of surgical tape across the lips. Start with the centre only. Build tolerance over a week. Not for everyone — if you have nasal obstruction or sleep apnoea, consult a physician first.

The Correction

Simple. Not easy. But simple.

1
Right now
Close your mouth
Lips together. Tongue resting on the roof of the mouth — not the teeth. This is the correct resting position. Hold it. Breathe through the nose. Notice it feels slightly more effortful. That's adaptation beginning.
2
During exercise
Nasal only
It feels harder at first. That is your body adapting to more efficient oxygen delivery. Within 2–3 weeks exercise feels easier than it did breathing through your mouth. McKeown's Oxygen Advantage documents this in elite athletes.
3
Model it
For your children
Children copy what they see. If you breathe nasally — consistently, visibly — they will adopt the pattern. This webinar is the most valuable thing you can do for your child's airway development tonight. Then show them.
📖

McKeown, P — The Oxygen Advantage: Nasal breathing during exercise improves performance, recovery and stress resilience. Within weeks of switching — measurable physiological improvement.
