Breathwork
5 · 5 · Coherence · HRV
Resonance
Five seconds in, five seconds out. Six cycles per minute. Creates coherence between heart rhythm and nervous system. Use to settle after a hard day, before a difficult conversation, or to come back to yourself.

4 · 4 · 4 · 4 · Focus
Box
Equal phases of inhale, hold, exhale, hold. Provides structure and mild activation when the mind is scattered. Before high-pressure situations, important decisions, or when you need to focus fast.
Open tool →
4 · 7 · 8 · Downregulate
Extended Exhale
The long exhale directly activates the vagal brake. Before sleep, after sustained stress, when anxiety is running high and you need to fully come down.
Open tool →
4 · 2 · 8 · Acute
Physiological Sigh
Double inhale through the nose, long exhale. The strongest evidence for rapid parasympathetic recovery. Use in the moment — a spike of stress, before you walk into something hard, when nothing else is fast enough.
