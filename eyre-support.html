When to seek
professional support

The tools offered by EYRE have real value — and real limits. Knowing when to step beyond them is part of using them well.

These tools are for self-regulation support and psychoeducation only. They are not a clinical assessment, therapeutic treatment, or substitute for professional mental health care. If you are unsure whether what you are experiencing requires professional attention, err on the side of making the call.
Stop and seek support if you notice
These tools consistently increase your distress, anxiety, or discomfort rather than reducing it.
Stress or anxiety continues without improvement despite regular use over time.
You experience flashbacks, dissociation, or trauma responses during or after use.
Panic attacks occur that do not resolve on their own.
You are having thoughts of self-harm or suicide.
You feel significantly worse after repeated use of any exercise.
Start with your GP
Your general practitioner is the right first point of contact. They can assess what is happening, rule out physical contributors, and refer you to the appropriate professional. You do not need to have a crisis to make that appointment.
