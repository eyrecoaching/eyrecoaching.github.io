About These Tools

EYRE Coaching provides a suite of digital self-regulation tools including breathwork guides, nervous system state identification resources, and psychoeducational exercises grounded in published research and established clinical frameworks.

These tools are designed for personal self-regulation support and psychoeducation. They draw on the following evidence bases and frameworks:

—
Polyvagal Theory, developed by Dr Stephen Porges and adapted for clinical practice by Deb Dana, LCSW (Rhythm of Regulation). Polyvagal Theory is a widely used clinical framework; certain aspects remain subject to ongoing scientific debate.
—
Resonance and coherence breathing research, including work by Lehrer, Vaschillo, and Gevirtz on heart rate variability biofeedback.
—
Cyclic sighing (physiological sigh) research: Balban, Huberman et al. (2023), Cell Reports Medicine, Stanford University.
—
Extended exhale (4-7-8) breathing: Dr Andrew Weil, adapted from pranayama tradition.
—
Box breathing: established practice, adapted from military and clinical stress management protocols.
—
The concept of glimmers: developed by Deb Dana, LCSW, from her published work on Polyvagal Theory.
2
Scope and Limitations

These tools are for self-regulation education and support only. They are not clinical interventions, therapeutic treatments, diagnostic instruments, or medical devices.

—
The nervous system state identification tool is a self-reflection prompt. It is not a validated psychometric instrument and does not constitute a clinical assessment or diagnosis.
—
Breathwork duration and timing recommendations are based on published research and are provided as general guidance. Individual responses vary.
—
State-to-breathwork recommendations are informed by published research and clinical frameworks. Where specific pairings are clinical inferences rather than directly tested protocols, this is noted within the tool.
—
These tools do not replace the advice, assessment, or treatment provided by a registered psychologist, psychiatrist, general practitioner, or other licensed health professional.
3
Who These Tools Are For

These tools are intended for adults aged 18 and over seeking general self-regulation support. They are not designed for, and should not be used as, treatment for diagnosed mental health conditions, trauma responses, or medical conditions without the oversight of a qualified health professional.

If you are currently experiencing a mental health crisis, acute trauma response, or symptoms of a serious psychological or medical condition, please seek professional support immediately.

4
Breathwork and Medical Clearance

Breathwork exercises involve deliberate alteration of normal breathing patterns. The following individuals should consult a medical professional before use:

—
Persons with cardiovascular conditions, hypertension, or respiratory conditions including asthma or COPD.
—
Persons who are pregnant.
—
Persons with a history of seizures, fainting, or dissociative episodes.
—
Persons currently experiencing acute anxiety, panic disorder, or PTSD.

If you experience lightheadedness, dizziness, chest pain, or significant discomfort during any exercise, stop immediately and breathe normally. If symptoms persist, seek medical attention.

5
When to Seek Professional Support

Stop using these tools and speak with a qualified health professional if:

—
Using these tools consistently increases your distress, anxiety, or discomfort rather than reducing it.
—
Stress or anxiety continues without improvement despite regular use over time.
—
You experience flashbacks, dissociation, or trauma responses during or after use.
—
You experience panic attacks that do not resolve.
—
You are having thoughts of self-harm or suicide.

Your GP is a good first point of contact.
